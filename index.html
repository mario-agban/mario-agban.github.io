LA Crime Project

An analysis of crime trends in Los Angeles from 2010 to 2023

My project delves into a comprehensive analysis of crime statistics in Los Angeles from 2010 to 2023, offering valuable insights into the evolving landscape of crime in the city. By examining detailed crime descriptions, the demographics of victims, and the types of weapons used, we aim to shed light on patterns and trends that can inform public safety strategies. Understanding the racial dynamics of victimization and the prevalence of different weapons helps pinpoint areas of heightened risk and provides a clearer picture of crime’s impact on various communities. This analysis not only highlights the most dangerous hours for criminal activity but also serves as a crucial resource for advising the public on how to protect themselves. By identifying peak times for crime and offering practical safety tips, my effort is to empower residents to make informed decisions and take proactive measures to enhance their personal security.

  • Organization: Modern Data Lab
  • Tech: Excel • Tableau
  • Link: Public Tableau Dashboard
  • Date: July 2024 - August 2024

uMaps

An innovative indoor mapping iOS app designed to be fully integrated with Google Maps.

0's represent walkable areas, 1's walls, and 9's the computed path.

uMaps tackled an unsolved problem in the geospatial realm: indoor navigation. While other team members worked with Swift and Google Maps API, I focused on the backend. I implemented an object-oriented approach to representing building data and used the A* algorithm to efficiently find paths. Additional functionality included accessbility features and multi-floor capability.

Accenture Project

Advising a social media client on their content creation strategy

    Social Buzz is a social media client that reached a massive scale within recent years and does not have the resources internally to handle it. They want to see “an analysis of their content categories showing the top 5 categories with the largest popularity”. Here is the 5 steps I took to tackle this problem:

  • 1. Data understanding - the key to success on any data project is to understand the data in detail. So we took the time to understand the data model and domain of your business.
  • 2. Data cleaning - after understanding your business, we then cleaned the available datasets and thought about what an ideal dataset should look like for this problem.
  • 3. Data modelling - After ensuring the data was clean for analysis, we needed to process and model this data into a dataset that can precisely answer the business questions and produce the results needed.
  • 4. Data analysis - With our new dataset, we used our analytical expertise to uncover insights from this dataset and to produce visualizations to describe the insights.
  • 5. And finally we used these insights to unlock business decisions and to make recommendations on next steps.
